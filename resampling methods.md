# resampling methods

> [!summary] Quick definition
> Resampling methods approximate sampling distributions by repeatedly drawing or reassigning data from the observed sample or design.

## When it matters

Bootstrap, jackknife, permutation, and randomization methods are useful when analytic variance formulas are hard or when design-based inference is preferred. The resampling unit must match the data-generating or assignment structure.
