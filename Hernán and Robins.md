# Hernán and Robins

> [!summary]
> Miguel Hernán and James Robins. *Causal Inference: What If* (2020) — comprehensive textbook on causal inference from an epidemiological perspective, covering g-methods (g-formula, IPW, g-estimation), time-varying treatments, and mediation.

## Key Topics Covered

| Part | Focus | Key Methods |
|------|-------|-------------|
| I | Causal inference without models | Randomization, confounding, standardization |
| II | Causal inference with models | Outcome regression, IPW, g-formula |
| III | Time-varying treatments | Marginal structural models, g-estimation, SNMM |

## Distinctive Features

- **Epidemiological perspective**: Emphasizes time-varying confounders, censoring, and longitudinal designs
- **G-methods**: Comprehensive treatment of the "g-formula" (standardization), IPW, and structural nested models
- **No-modeling vs. modeling**: Clear distinction between identification and estimation
- **Sequential exchangeability**: Framework for time-varying treatments with feedback
- Freely available online at [https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/miguel-hernan/causal-inference-book/](https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/miguel-hernan/causal-inference-book/)

> [!note]
> Complements [[Angrist and Pischke]] (economics/quasi-experiments) and [[Imbens and Rubin]] (potential outcomes theory). Particularly strong on longitudinal and time-varying treatment settings.
