# Imbens and Rubin

> [!summary]
> Guido Imbens and Donald Rubin. *Causal Inference for Statistics, Social, and Biomedical Sciences* (2015) — comprehensive textbook on the potential outcomes framework, covering randomization, matching, propensity score methods, and IV.

## Key Topics Covered

| Part | Focus | Key Concepts |
|------|-------|-------------|
| I | Classical randomized experiments | Fisher, Neyman, [[potential outcomes]], [[Average Treatment Effect (ATE)]] |
| II | Regular assignment mechanisms | [[Unconfoundedness]], [[propensity score]], matching, [[Inverse Probability Weighting (IPW)|IPW]] |
| III | Noncompliance and IV | [[Local Average Treatment Effect (LATE)|LATE]], [[Instrumental Variables (IV)]], principal stratification |
| IV | Advanced topics | [[Regression Discontinuity Design (RDD)|RDD]], mechanisms, mediation |

## Distinctive Features

- **Potential outcomes framework**: Rigorous formalization of Rubin's causal model
- **Assignment mechanisms**: Emphasis on how treatment is assigned (randomization vs. selection on observables)
- **Principal stratification**: Framework for handling noncompliance, truncation, and mediation
- **Bayesian perspective**: Incorporates Bayesian inference throughout

> [!note]
> Canonical reference for the Rubin causal model. More formal and mathematical than [[Angrist and Pischke]]. Complements [[Hernán and Robins]] (g-methods, time-varying) and Morgan & Winship (sociological perspective).
