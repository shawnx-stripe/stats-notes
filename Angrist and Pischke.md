# Angrist and Pischke

> [!summary]
> Joshua Angrist and Jörn-Steffen Pischke. *Mostly Harmless Econometrics* (2009) — foundational textbook on the design-based approach to causal inference (IV, DiD, RDD). Also *Mastering 'Metrics* (2015) for undergraduates.

## Key contributions

MHE popularized the "credibility revolution" in applied microeconomics, emphasizing transparent research designs over structural modeling. Core message: focus on clearly identified causal effects from quasi-experiments rather than elaborate parametric assumptions. The book covers IV (LATE framework), DiD, RDD, and standard errors with minimal mathematical prerequisites, making design-based inference accessible to practitioners.

> [!note]
> Chapter 3 on IV is essential reading for understanding [[Local Average Treatment Effect (LATE)|LATE]], compliers, and the distinction between average treatment effects. Chapter 8 covers the pitfalls of nonlinear models in causal inference.
