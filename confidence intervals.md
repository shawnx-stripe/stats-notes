# confidence intervals

> [!summary] Quick definition
> A confidence interval is a procedure that covers the target parameter with a stated long-run frequency under repeated sampling.

## When it matters

Intervals communicate uncertainty better than a single point estimate. Their validity depends on matching the interval construction to the estimator, dependence structure, and sample size.
